Park context

Accessibility, surfaces, and national park expectations.

Sarah’s Vineyard is a public‑facing business, but it also sits within a network of working farms in Cuyahoga Valley National Park. Surfaces and accessibility reflect that reality.

  • Many farm sites in the park—including Sarah’s Vineyard—have turf, gravel, or mulch surfaces that can be muddy after rain. Wheelchair accessibility can be limited and varies by season.
  • Table service is offered in the tasting room, deck, and butterfly garden. Bar service is offered at the pavilion and surrounding picnic tables.
  • Children must be supervised and seated next to their parent or guardian at all times, inside and outside, for everyone’s safety.
  • Outside food is not permitted. By Ohio law, all alcohol consumed on site must be purchased through Sarah’s Vineyard.
  • Dogs on leash are welcome at outdoor picnic tables; please clean up after your pet and be mindful of other guests.

Blossom Music Center tips

How to combine a show with a winery visit.

  • On concert days, seating is first‑come/first‑served—no seating reservations are taken.
  • Outdoor seating may be available; bringing a lawn chair can make waits more comfortable.
  • Guests can order wine or beverages while waiting. To order food, you must be seated.
  • Food arrival times may differ between the pizza kitchen and main kitchen, especially before shows.
  • Before select concerts, a streamlined “Quick Bites” menu helps keep service moving. See the Food section for how that works.
  • No outside food or alcohol (except cake/cupcakes). No tailgating in the parking lot; no Blossom parking is allowed.

Guidelines

Quick visit checklist

A short list based on National Park Service guidance and the winery’s policies—so first‑time visitors know what to expect.

Before you come

  • Check the winery’s hours page for the latest hours and any special closures.
  • Decide whether your party is 8+ and, if so, call ahead for a reservation.
  • Plan for turf/gravel surfaces and weather—comfortable shoes are a good idea.

When you arrive

  • Choose where you’d like to sit: tasting room, deck, butterfly garden, pavilion, or picnic tables (seasonal).
  • Remember that table service is offered in the tasting room, deck, and garden; bar service is at the pavilion/picnic tables.
  • Keep children close and seated; keep dogs on leash at outdoor tables only.

Policies that matter

  • No outside food is permitted, with limited exceptions for certain private bookings.
  • By Ohio law, all alcohol consumed on site must be purchased through Sarah’s Vineyard.
  • Groups larger than 15 incur a 20% gratuity; loft rentals are available for groups of 20–40.

Gift certificates

Gift certificates are sold in store and can be purchased by phone. Call (330) 929-8057 for details.
