Ready-to-Lay Hens

Golden Buff/Comets from Eagle Nest,organic ration, beaks intact.

"Ready-to-lay" means the bird is close to egg production,ideal if you want eggs soon without brooding chicks.

Each spring we offer ready-to-lay pullets at ~16 weeks. This option skips the highest-risk stage (first weeks of life) and puts your focus on housing, feed, predator-proofing, and daily routine.

Readiness checklist

  • Coop ready before birds arrive: secure, dry, ventilated, predator-resistant
  • Plan for winter: wind protection, dry bedding, consistent feed
  • Feed that matches production stage (laying birds need calcium for shells)
  • Biosecurity mindset; give birds time to settle after the move

Transplants

Healthy starts, ready when conditions are right.

Transplants compress time. In Northeast Ohio that matters,plants start protected, then go in when conditions are right.

How we grow them

Organic seed (with a few exceptions) and organic potting mix from Tilth or Ohio Earth Food. We're exploring certified organic mineral sprays for micronutrients and seeding companion plants with our vegetable transplants to promote healthy soil biology in each soil block.

Three jobs of a transplant page

  • What's offered: tomatoes/peppers, greens, herbs, succession plants for fall.
  • When: Zone 6 guidance; frost dates vary,check before you plant.
  • Hardening off: Acclimate seedlings to sun, wind, and temperature gradually. Start sheltered, increase exposure day by day; transplant on a cloudy afternoon or late day.

Tomato and pepper transplants are typically ready Memorial Day weekend. Need a specific variety or quantity? Let us know!
